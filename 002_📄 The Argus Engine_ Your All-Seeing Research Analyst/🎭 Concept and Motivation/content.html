In ancient myth, Argus Panoptes was a giant with one hundred eyes, a vigilant guardian who could see everything from every angle at once. This project challenges you to build a modern version of this all-seeing power: The Argus Engine.The real challenge for any researcher, analyst, or student isn't reading one document—it's synthesizing the truth from a dozen. This project moves beyond a simple Q&A bot to create an AI research assistant that investigates an entire collection of documents simultaneously. It uses its many "eyes" to uncover hidden connections, flag conflicting information, and weave scattered facts into a single, cohesive story.Why It's a Great ChallengeThe motivation for this project is to tackle the overwhelming problem of information synthesis. You’re not just building a document reader; you're building an AI that creates new knowledge from existing texts.By building this integrated system, you will:Tackle Information Overload: You'll design a powerful tool that saves users hours of manual cross-referencing, allowing them to ask complex, comparative questions and get synthesized answers instantly.Master Advanced RAG: This project will challenge you to build a sophisticated Retrieval-Augmented Generation pipeline. The key is managing metadata to ensure your engine can not only find information but also cite its sources from across the entire document collection.Focus on Synthesis, Not Just Summarization: This is the core of the challenge. You will engineer prompts that push the LLM beyond simple fact retrieval and encourage it to compare, contrast, and generate novel insights that aren't explicitly stated in any single source.The goal is to build an application that demonstrates the true power of RAG for deep analysis, revealing a future where AI can help us find the hidden truths buried in our data.
