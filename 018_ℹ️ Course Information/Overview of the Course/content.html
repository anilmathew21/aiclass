Large Language Models (LLMs) have fundamentally reshaped the landscape of Natural Language Processing (NLP), enabling breakthroughs in text generation, reasoning, and human-computer interaction. These models underpin cutting-edge AI applications, from conversational agents to advanced knowledge retrieval systems. However, as their capabilities expand, so do the challenges associated with scalability, interpretability, and ethical considerations.This course is designed to provide a comprehensive foundation in modern language modeling, covering both the theoretical underpinnings and practical applications of LLMs. Beginning with traditional NLP approaches such as statistical models and embeddings, the curriculum progresses through transformers, instruction-based models, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), agentic AI, fine-tuning strategies, and evaluation methods.Participants will gain hands-on experience with state-of-the-art techniques through structured coding exercises, real-world case studies, and interactive discussions. The course will not only prepare students to understand and implement advanced LLM architectures but also equip them with the necessary skills to adapt and optimize these models for industry applications and academic research.Beyond technical proficiency, the course also explores critical topics such as security, and ethical AI deployment, ensuring that participants are well-versed in the broader implications of LLM technology.By the end of the course, students will have a deep understanding of the inner workings of LLMs, their strengths and limitations, and the methodologies required to leverage them effectively in various domains. Discussion on how deep neural networks extend these ideas by capturing richer word relationships and contextual meaning through embeddings. Transformer Architecture, BERT, and GPTUnderstanding self-attention and the transformer mechanism. Study of BERT’s bidirectional architecture, masked language modeling, and fine-tuning strategies. Examination of GPT’s autoregressive nature and its impact on generative AI.Instruction-based Models: From GPT to ChatGPTHow instruction-tuned models like ChatGPT differ from standard LLMs. The role of RLHF in optimizing responses. Practical strategies for prompt engineering, including zero-shot, few-shot, and chain-of-thought prompting.Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and Advanced Retrieval TechniquesHow RAG enhances LLMs with external knowledge retrieval. Implementation of retrieval mechanisms using document chunking, embeddings, and search techniques. Exploration of self-improving RAG models and their applications in real-world AI systems.AgentsUnderstanding AI agents powered by LLMs, from task-based automation to multi-agent collaboration. Applications of agentic frameworks in interactive and decision-making systems.Fine-Tuning LLMs Using LoRAOverview of LoRA as an efficient fine-tuning approach for adapting models to specialized domains. Introduction to qLoRA for optimizing fine-tuning performance on resource-constrained hardware.Advances in LLM Efficiency and DevelopmentExploration of state-of-the-art techniques that push the boundaries of LLM performance and usability. Topics include mixture of experts (ME), latent attention as an emerging approach to more efficient context handling, and caching/retrieval methods to extend effective context length and reduce inference costs. Discussion of how these innovations shape the future of LLM deployment at scale.
